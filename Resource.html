1) Automatic counting and measuring procedure for mobile laboratory organisms using contracting backgrounds

Microcosm experiments usually requires laborous counting and measuring procedures. For many laborotory model organisms such as the Collembola and soil mites, sampling population and trait dynamics requires extra efforts as their movements are prone to small disturbances. Using image-processing tool and automatic repeating procedure, I developed an automatic counting procedure with ImageJ plugin.

A contracting background can be removed using at least three photos, and further steps involve area alignment, particle filtering, etc, accroding to standard ImageJ processes. Using mutiple loops coded in Java, this process can automatically process thousands of pictures and return csv data at the same time!

A pre-coded ImageJ plugin for measuring number and size can be Download and modified by users.

2) Dataset: Density-dependent regulation on trait response

Environmental variability is a key driver of life-history strategy, yet its effects are often complex and context-dependent. My research explores how fluctuating environments shape life-history strategies, including development, survival, and reproduction, across interacting species. Rather than responding to average conditions, organisms must cope with variability, extremes, and the timing of stress events. I am particularly interested in how these factors generate stage-specific and species-specific responses, and how repeated exposure to environmental fluctuations can drive adaptive change. This work highlights the importance of variability—not just mean change—in shaping ecological and evolutionary dynamics.
